Background

I am an Undergraduate Computer Science student at the University of Michigan, focusing on robotics and autonomous systems. I currently work in the Manipulation and Machine Intelligence (MMINT) Lab, where I research robotic manipulation and control. My broader research interests span robotic perception, control systems, and machine learning applications in robotics.

Research Interests

I am particularly interested in the intersection of robotics and machine learning, focusing on:

  • Robotic manipulation and control
  • Model-based planning and control
  • Hardware-software integration in robotics
  • Machine learning applications in autonomous systems

Experience

Viam (May 2024 - August 2024)

At Viam in New York, I worked on expanding hardware support for robotics applications. My main projects included developing a custom pin control library for the RaspberryPi5, supporting features like PWM, GPIO, and pull up/down resistors. I also integrated MIMO capabilities into Viam's control systems package to enhance PID controller performance.

EverestLabs (May 2023 - August 2023)

While at EverestLabs, I focused on improving robotic perception and manipulation for waste sorting systems. I prototyped six different algorithms for determining optimal contact surfaces for FANUC robots and built infrastructure for object height data collection and depth map generation through preliminary testing.

Zipline (May 2022 - August 2022)

During my time at Zipline, I worked on drone serviceability solutions and site integration. I designed and built prototypes for various drone serviceability concepts and ground support equipment. My work led to being listed as an inventor on two patents filed in 2023.

Georgia Tech Biorobotics Lab (September 2021 - May 2022)

As an undergraduate researcher, I worked on two major projects:

  • A vascular access cannulation device, collaborating with Emory School of Medicine to improve vein visibility during IV insertion for hypovolemic patients
  • The AutoSpine Robot project, where I designed a body-conforming mounting system with 6 degrees of freedom for performing radiofrequency ablation procedures

Zipline (June 2021 - August 2021)

In my first role at Zipline, I focused on improving delivery accuracy for drone operations. I created standardized testing procedures for mass testing and package behavior analysis, investigated outlier delivery cases, and implemented design changes that improved drop accuracy by 30%.
